# ⚡ Heat Pump and Air Conditioning Load Forecasting

## 📖 Project Overview

This project investigates how increasing adoption of heat pumps and air conditioning systems may affect future electricity demand in Calgary. The objective is to evaluate multiple methodologies for estimating additional electrical load under climate change and electrification scenarios.

🎓 Completed as part of a Summer 2026 NSERC Undergraduate Research Award at the University of Calgary.

---

## 🛠️ Technologies Used

*  Microsoft Excel
*  Engineering and physics-based modelling
*  Weather and temperature datasets
*  Electricity demand datasets
*  Research papers and technical reports
*  GitHub for version control and project organization

---

## ✨ Features

The Excel workbook includes:

*  Multiple forecasting methodologies organized into separate worksheets
*  Input validation and error checking
*  User-defined system parameters
*  Heating load calculations
*  Cooling load calculations
*  COP-based heat pump performance calculations
*  Methodology comparison tables
*  Source references and supporting documentation

---

## 🗂️ Datasets Used

*  Calgary hourly temperature data
*  AESO electricity demand data
*  Heat pump adoption and market data
*  Published research papers and technical reports
*  Building energy and load profile datasets

---

## 🔨 Development Process

1.  Reviewed literature on heat pump and air conditioning load forecasting.
2.  Collected and organized weather, load, and market datasets.
3.  Developed multiple forecasting methodologies.
4.  Implemented calculations in Excel.
5.  Tested formulas and input validation.
6.  Compared methodologies against source literature and engineering assumptions.
7.  Organized results for future analysis and validation.

---

## 🚧 Challenges Faced

*  Finding datasets that aligned in both time and geographic location.
*  Limited availability of localized heat pump electricity consumption data.
*  Reconciling differences between methodologies found in the literature.
*  Managing uncertainty associated with future adoption rates and climate conditions.
*  Converting theoretical equations into user-friendly Excel tools.
