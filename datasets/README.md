# Datasets

## Overview

This directory contains all datasets collected for the **Heat Pump Electricity Demand Forecasting** project. These datasets provide the foundation for developing, validating, and comparing the methodologies used to estimate the electrical demand of residential heat pumps under different operating conditions and future electrification scenarios.

---

## Folder Structure

### Weather Data

**Purpose**
- Historical outdoor temperature data used throughout the project.

**Application**
- Estimate heating demand
- Analyze heat pump performance under varying weather conditions
- Support hourly electrical demand calculations

---

### Electricity Load Data

**Purpose**
- Historical electricity demand data for Alberta.

**Application**
- Compare estimated heat pump demand with existing grid demand
- Evaluate the impact of electrification on the electrical grid
- Support future demand forecasting

---

### Heat Pump Data

**Purpose**
- Heat pump performance data, including COP values and system characteristics.

**Application**
- Convert heating demand into electrical energy consumption
- Compare engineering methodologies
- Analyze heat pump efficiency

---

### Building Data

**Purpose**
- Residential building energy reports and heating load information.

**Application**
- Estimate annual and monthly heating demand
- Validate modelling methodologies
- Compare model predictions with case studies

---

### Market Data

**Purpose**
- Heat pump adoption statistics and electrification reports.

**Application**
- Develop future adoption scenarios
- Estimate additional electrical demand resulting from increased heat pump adoption

---

### Reference Reports

**Purpose**
- Supporting literature, technical reports, and research papers.

**Application**
- Validate engineering assumptions
- Support methodology development
- Compare results with published research

---

## Research Assessment

Each dataset contributes a different component required to model residential heat pump electricity demand. Weather data provides environmental conditions, building data defines heating requirements, heat pump data describes system performance, electricity load data enables grid-level analysis, and market data supports future forecasting scenarios.

Although no single dataset contains every variable required, combining these sources provides a comprehensive foundation for the methodologies developed in this project.
